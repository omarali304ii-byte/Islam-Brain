# Cielito Egypt — 360 Research Report
*Base 360 · July 2026 · client-safe. Every figure traces to a captured source; ranges and gaps are labeled honestly.*

## 1. Who Cielito is
Egyptian women's fashion brand (Shopify D2C), founded ~2021 by Lolwa Awad. Started in handmade leather footwear (heels, mules, sandals, boots, ballerinas, slippers), extended into bags and occasion apparel (kimonos, sets, dresses). 250 live products, EGP 400–7,600 (typical ~1,200). Positioning today: accessible, locally-made, design-led — but the message has drifted (three taglines, a discontinued signature line).

## 2. The market it plays in
Egypt's footwear market is roughly EGP 31.4bn in retail value (2025, ~+9% year on year; a separate source frames it near $635M — we treat the size as an estimate range, not a single figure). Women are about 52% of demand, e-commerce is still under-penetrated (~90% of sales remain offline), and the risk everyone faces is squeezed purchasing power. The winners are local, affordable, socially-native brands — which is exactly Cielito's lane, led at scale by Dejavu.

## 3. The core diagnosis: a broken engine
- **Reach:** 88,903 Instagram followers, verified, 1,368 posts — but owned posts earn a **median of 3 likes** (engagement rate ~0.006%). Content from customers and creators reaches up to **5,930 likes / 124,937 views — ~190× the brand's own median.**
- **Language:** the brand's best posts on both platforms are in Egyptian Arabic; 16 of 17 recent captions were English.
- **Conversion:** no WhatsApp path anywhere, despite a check-on-delivery model that already runs like a conversation.
- **TikTok:** 584 videos posted for 7,608 followers — effort without a strategy; identity is mixed with founder-journey content.
- **Catalog & site:** 128 of 250 products untyped, duplicate collections, 38% on permanent discount; mobile site speed 55/100 (desktop 98, SEO perfect).

## 4. What it's sitting on (the assets)
A verified 89K audience, a **proven word-of-mouth flywheel** (12+ creators including the founder's own active account), a check-before-you-pay trust mechanic, an articulate founder story, and a **press-validated fruit-leather innovation** — the one differentiator no rival owns — currently missing from every live surface.

## 5. Where it can win (positioning)
White space in the market: design-led **+** accessible **+** a sustainability/innovation story. Dejavu owns volume; premium designers (Jayda Hany, Bulga, Ramla) own craft-luxury; nobody at mid-price owns Cielito's potential slice. The move is to contest that niche, not to out-shout Dejavu on reach.

## 6. The plan (90 days, mostly no ad spend)
Repair the engine first: WhatsApp bridge → catalog cleanup + Arabic-first content → creator program → mobile speed → founder-gated positioning. Concentrate commercial pushes into the demand windows the catalog proves (Ramadan/Eid, summer/Sahel, winter boots, Black Friday). Full detail in the strategy and the 90-day calendar.

## 7. What we don't yet know (and how to close it)
Revenue/conversion (needs your data), the true quality of the 89K followers (needs an audit + your Insights), Facebook and rival benchmarks (a data-collection pass), the fruit-leather status and the second domain (one founder conversation). None of these block the plan; all are listed with a closing route in the next-steps menu.

## 8. Confidence & method
Diagnosis is built on primary captures: the live site, the full 250-product catalog, 120 Instagram/TikTok posts, and site-health scans — each claim graded and sourced. Market sizing is an estimate range. Revenue impact is deliberately left "to be baselined" rather than invented. No absence is claimed from a source we couldn't reach.
